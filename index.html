Certification identification

The DPP uses a three-layer review model: external certificates evidence the claim, while Line 1, Quality Control and Final Audit close the internal DPP case sequentially. Dates are recorded only when each reviewer closes their own step.

Role-based access and closure workflow

This is the concrete KYC / CLM-inspired control: reviewers have different access rights and the case only moves forward when the previous reviewer closes their own step. The review date is recorded at closure.

Line 1 Analyst

Collects documents, matches UID / batch UID and confirms the claim has minimum evidence.

Status: Open Review date: Not closed yet Access: Edit checklist + close Line 1

Quality Control Analyst

Reviews Line 1 evidence quality, challenges gaps and confirms the confidence level.

Status: Locked Review date: Waiting for Line 1 closure Access: Review + challenge + close QC

Final Audit Checks

Checks end-to-end evidence, auditability and whether the public DPP claim can be released.

Status: Locked Review date: Waiting for QC closure Access: Final approval + publication gate

Accessing as Line 1 Analyst

Can edit the documentation checklist, verify product UID / batch UID match, attach certificates and close the initial evidence review.

  • Edit identification and documentation checklist.
  • Confirm certificate presence and validity date.
  • Close Line 1 only; cannot approve final DPP publication.
